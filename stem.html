STEM Program

As the 21st century progresses, young people must be equipped with the knowledge and skills to solve tough problems, gather and evaluate evidence, and make sense of information. Students learn these types of skills by studying Science, Technology, Engineering and Math - subjects collectively known as STEM.

Currently, few students in the United States seek knowledge in STEM fields, and the number of teachers skilled in those subjects is inadequate. Both Nevada and national workforce projections suggest that, over the next decade, the majority of jobs will require strong talent in key STEM skills. Lovelace Technical Academy's STEM programs bring together state agencies and school districts to focus on STEM education, with goals to strengthen instruction and academics, build relationships with industry experts and the business community, prepare students for STEM careers, and create best practices to use in other school districts.
